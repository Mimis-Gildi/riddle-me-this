# THREAD 2: US SOF training of Azov, 2015-2018

## Claim 2A — A US Special Operations unit trained Azov during this period

**Vadim's framing:** "klemo was in Delta SOCOM volunteering detachment tasked to train Azov."

**Verdict:** Defensible at the *partner-force* level, but **"Delta" is almost certainly a memory conflation**. The publicly-attested US SOF unit doing partner-force work with Ukrainian forces in this window is the **10th Special Forces Group (Airborne)**, headquartered at Fort Carson with 1st Battalion forward-stationed at Panzer Kaserne, Stuttgart, under SOCEUR — exactly the unit that wears the patch the author has identified. Delta Force (1st SFOD-D / CAG) is a Tier 1 direct-action and counterterrorism unit; they do not run FID rotations with partner-force light infantry, which is what Azov training would have required.

The public record on *direct US-SOF training of Azov specifically in 2015-2018* is thin because the **Conyers loophole period (June 2015 ban → late 2015 ban-narrowing → 2017 reinstatement → ongoing leaks)** meant any such training was either (a) before the ban took effect, (b) routed through general programs (JMTG-U / Operation Fearless Guardian / National Academy of the Army at Yavoriv) where Azov members ended up in the trainee pool without being specifically named, or (c) deliberately not reported.

## Claim 2B — The Conyers amendment restricted US aid/training to Azov

**Vadim's framing:** Implicit context for the timeline — Azov was eventually walled off from official US training.

**Verdict:** Strong, but the timeline is messier than commonly summarized. The "2018 NDAA ban" is a popular shorthand but the legal history runs:

- **June 11 2015:** House unanimously passes the Conyers-Yoho amendment to the Defense Appropriations bill, prohibiting US training of and arms transfers to the Azov Battalion. ([C-SPAN clip of Conyers floor speech](https://www.c-span.org/clip/us-house-of-representatives/user-clip-rep-john-conyers-introduces-amendment-to-prohibit-the-azov-battalion-from-receiving-us-arms-and-training/4942324) | [Congressional Record](https://www.congress.gov/congressional-record/volume-161/issue-92/house-section/article/H4117-2))
- **Late 2015 / early 2016:** In conference negotiations, the Azov-specific language was stripped out; only the MANPADS prohibition (Iraq, Syria, Ukraine) survived. So for FY16, **there was no Azov-specific statutory ban**. ([The Nation — "Congress Has Removed a Ban on Funding Neo-Nazis From Its Year-End Spending Bill"](https://www.thenation.com/article/politics/congress-has-removed-a-ban-on-funding-neo-nazis-from-its-year-end-spending-bill/))
- **March 2018:** The Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2018 included the 23-word Azov-specific prohibition: *"None of the funds made available by this Act may be used to provide arms, training, or other assistance to the Azov Battalion."* This is the statutory text Vadim and others remember as "the Conyers amendment" — and it carried forward in successor appropriations.
- **June 10-12 2024:** State Department lifted the prohibition with respect to the *12th Special Forces Azov Brigade* (a 2023 Ukrainian National Guard re-formation), holding that the original 2014 militia "disbanded in 2015" and the current unit is legally distinct. ([CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/amp/news/ukraine-azov-brigade-weapons-training-us-ban-lifted/) | [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2024/06/12/europe/us-weapons-azov-brigade-ukraine-intl) | [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/u-s-lifts-ban-on-providing-weapons-and-training-for-ukraines-controversial-azov-brigade))

**Sources:**

1. [C-SPAN — Rep. Conyers introduces the Azov amendment, June 10 2015](https://www.c-span.org/clip/us-house-of-representatives/user-clip-rep-john-conyers-introduces-amendment-to-prohibit-the-azov-battalion-from-receiving-us-arms-and-training/4942324) — Primary source: the actual House floor introduction.
2. [The Nation — "Congress Has Removed a Ban on Funding Neo-Nazis From Its Year-End Spending Bill" (Jan 2016)](https://www.thenation.com/article/politics/congress-has-removed-a-ban-on-funding-neo-nazis-from-its-year-end-spending-bill/) — Documents the conference-committee removal of the Azov-specific language from the FY16 omnibus.
3. [PBS NewsHour — "U.S. lifts ban on providing weapons and training for Ukraine's controversial Azov Brigade" (June 2024)](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/u-s-lifts-ban-on-providing-weapons-and-training-for-ukraines-controversial-azov-brigade) — Mainstream confirmation of the 2024 lift and the State Department's "different unit" rationale.
4. [The Intercept — Azov training despite the ban (June 22 2024)](https://theintercept.com/2024/06/22/ukraine-azov-battalion-us-training-ban/) — Establishes that the *de facto* enforcement of the ban was imperfect, with Azov members ending up in US-instructor-staffed coursework via the National Academy of the Army channel.

**Complications:**
- The "Conyers amendment" is *not* a 2018 NDAA provision; it's an appropriations-bill rider (Consolidated Appropriations Act 2018). If Vadim writes "2018 NDAA," a sharp reader will catch it. Cleaner to say "the 2018 appropriations Azov ban" or "the Conyers-era prohibition."

## Claim 2C — Azov's institutional character changed across formations

**Vadim's framing:** "The original, still a bit nationalistic Azov" — the implication being that early Azov and current Azov are not the same beast.

**Verdict:** Strong, and Vadim's intuition matches the State Department's legal position. The transitions:

- **May 2014:** Azov Battalion founded as a volunteer militia under Andriy Biletsky, drawn from the ultranationalist Patriot of Ukraine network and the neo-Nazi-affiliated Social-National Assembly.
- **September-November 2014:** Reorganized to regiment status; formally enrolled in the National Guard of Ukraine November 11 2014. Biletsky left the unit in October 2014 to take up his parliamentary seat (and later founded the National Corps party in 2016).
- **January 2015:** Designated Special Operations Detachment "Azov."
- **2014-2022:** Operates as Azov Regiment under the National Guard. Subject of UN OHCHR report (2016) flagging international-humanitarian-law violations in Nov 2015 - Feb 2016.
- **February-May 2022:** The Mariupol siege; Azov becomes globally famous; surrender at Azovstal.
- **January 2023:** Reformed/expanded as 12th Special Forces Azov Brigade (still National Guard).
- **April 15 2025:** 1st Azov Corps stood up, formed on the basis of the Azov Brigade — the first full corps in the Ukrainian National Guard.
