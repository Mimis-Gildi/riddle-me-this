== 1: Mythos and Wonder-Models: "The fire truck is also the arsonist."

Anthropic sells a $125/million-token model to find bugs.{fn-mythos-price}

Meanwhile:

- 2.74x more vulnerabilities in AI-generated code vs human-written;{fn-veracode}
- 45% of AI-generated code introduces OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities;footnote:veracode[]
- Claude Code alone: 4% of all public GitHub commits;{fn-cc-commits}
- 1 in 5 organizations report serious incidents from AI-generated code;{fn-aikido}
- 69% of organizations found vulnerabilities in their AI-generated code.footnote:aikido[]

Everyone is speculating about a wonder-model that will obsolete developers and uproot all software.
There are three problems here.

- *First*, we know this tune -- OpenAI first.{fn-gpt2}
- *Second*, Anthropic, my favorite company, leaks like a sieve -- victim to its own success.
And what we spy is what we expected -- there are #no new breakthroughs in LLM architecture#,
so there is no Deus Ex Machina in Mythos.{fn-mythos-holes}
- And *third*, and perhaps the most important point, the breakthrough Mythos claims to own actually did happen 9-11 months ago.
But it wasn't some silly LLM. *Ukraine*, then *_Israel_*, closely followed by *_France_* and *_Great Britain_*,
are the nations at the forefront of AI applications by changing how we even think about software -- NOT the USA, regretfully.

Actual penetrators are much scarier.
First there is Ukrainian Delta, not just battlefield management, but also everything from logistics to geopolitics,
starting with AI-governed research; and then there is nothing else for a long time.
All of these mindbogglingly powerful AI aren't AI at all -- that IS the whole secret
-- they're absurdly massive *_Distributed Systems_*.

The breakthrough is in operational philosophy.
All of these nations have been deploying software vulnerability analysis as a *_weapon system_*
-- that is why Putin is diligently killing internet in Russia whilst hiding in his bunker.
Some American companies claimed proximity to Ukraine's "AI" as a marketing stunt
-- Ukraine doesn't let anyone in since 2024. Neither does Israel, France, GB, etc.
But don't worry, we also used something like this in our latest action against Ali Khamenei.{fn-khamenei}

Cybersecurity AI-Nukes have been weaponized for over a year now and your world didn't crash -- did it?
The important piece people miss here is that the power of these penetrators does NOT come from a model
-- that is IMPOSSIBLE. It comes from the analytic systems that use models; many models.
In the last section I will explain why and how. For now, it's about:

- *Centralized Control (tool):* Russia, China, USA -- not very effective but simple.
- *Distributed Control (team):* Iran, Baltics, Ukraine in Gulf -- effective but expensive.
- *Ecosystem Policy (theater):* Ukraine, France, Israel, GB -- changes everything!

Open Source Software is safest for exactly the same reason it is safest today -- its source is open
-- nothing changes *_unseen_* there. Proprietary software will temporarily get reamed and recover
-- this will be and has been a recurring theme already. The cycle will just tighten.

No single model will kill your company -- don't worry about that. Something else will.

== Stage Zero Monetization: Google Deterministic AI

A few months ago Google summoned their partner ecosystem to introduce "Deterministic AI"
-- I listen very carefully. Understood. Called people in business that implement AI.
It all went right over their heads.

Engineers began to make fun of "deterministic" with AI. Marketers struggled to spin product-market fit.
Everyone got confused. And things got quiet again. But Google is the adult in the room.
It isn't a new thing with them -- I remember this exact track in 2017 with my MATILDA framework.

Amazon is trying to give us a practical democratization with Bedrock -- but pragmatic is not yet possible.
Microsoft is still trying to sell "More Bugs for Less" model using proven "Don't look there."
Companies like Palantir are trying to package yesterday's ideas into futuristic "Business all you need is here."
And niche gods like Celonis know they're disrupted but don't understand HOW.
LLM shops only understand pushing a bigger model and hoping for users to discover monetization.
Everybody panics. Everybody except Google! I explained this in {google-play} -- three years later, same play, bigger stage.

Google's game is simple. And strategy is devastating. Their solution is to replace that DDD Aggregate,
a business service your team spent a year to write and years to "maintain" into submission, with a single drag-and-drop box.
That box encodes your domain logic -- the edge cases, the regulatory constraints, the competitive secret sauce
your business experts spent decades learning. Once it's in the box, Google has it. Not just yours -- *_everyone's_* in your vertical.
Google runs models across all of them, cross-pollinates insights no single company can match,
and offers you "better solutions" back. Now you can't leave -- the box knows your business better than your team does.
And their attitude to strategy is opposite to the "specialization" everyone else chose.
Like France and Ukraine in section one above, they chose *_Total War_* -- like Ukraine doesn't care about security tool alone,
but how it fits into an *_entire ecosystem_*, ending up dominating security and then some.
Google is poised to dominate every aspect of your life -- home with internet, cell phone, home security, family management;
business with drag and drop "we think for you" catering straight to lifer's mindset;
social life with AI-enabled search, integrated identity, services, and trinkets.
And all of this is powered by exactly the same hyperscaler beast and exactly the same maker community they brought into the fold *_first_*.

While the market plays HYPE, Google plays Python, literally slowly swallowing plebs whole.
I've been running deep analytics on these wise folks for years now -- I'm not a fan, though my business is a Google business,
`systems` domain and all -- I'm just in awe. GD Electric! Right in plain sight.
