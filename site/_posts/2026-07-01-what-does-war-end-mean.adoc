People who follow my OSINT and analytics work ask me *"WHEN will the war end?"*

Interestingly, not *"HOW will the war end?"*

No model can answer both and still be right tomorrow. Mathematics has a nasty habit of being absolute.
You can fix the variables and run out the clock -- then you get dates.
Or you can map the flows and let them converge -- then you get the outcome, not the timing.

Some people know WHEN. That requires access to real-time event data at scale.
({delta-url}[Delta,window=_blank], anyone?)

Many people know HOW. EU leadership knows. Ukrainian leadership knows.
Knows-knows -- nobody there is speculating.
POTUS was shown. Whether it registered is a separate question.

*I'm curious what Putin knows.*

== The Only Definition That Works

The {isw-url}[ISW framework,window=_blank] gives us the only mathematically sound definition:

[.text-center]
*Ukrainians stop killing Russians for good.* +
*OR* +
*Russians stop killing Ukrainians for good.*

This is an OR conjunction. Both conditions terminate the war. Neither pausing it, nor freezing it, nor negotiating it does -- those only suspend it. The model requires one side to stop permanently.

Simplified for non-mathematical readers:

- Ukraine ends → war stops.
- Russia ends → war stops.
- Neither ends → war continues.

Nothing else closes the equation.

== What Does Ukraine Need?

One thing: *stop killing my children now and forever.*

The "forever" clause is the hard part. It's also non-negotiable, because there is no version of history where Russia becomes a trustworthy neighbor. The only path Ukraine has calculated to enforce "forever" is to dismantle Russia as a threat.

Can Ukraine achieve this without foreign aid, against Russia plus North Korea plus Iran plus China?

That question was answered *yes* in October 2024 -- the day Zelensky wore the
{tshirt-url}["Make Russia Small Again" T-shirt,window=_blank].
The model at that point ran on Ukraine's internal annual budget of $37B against Russia's $250B.

Today {kse-url}[Ukraine is at $131.4B,window=_blank] and growing on recovering domestic industry.
{sipri-url}[Russia reports $238B,window=_blank] -- defence and security combined --
with a documented funding gap of $28-56B and the military requesting another $36B on top.

The weights have shifted. Still converging in the same direction.

Ukraine's war power is 63 times its 2020 figures.
Russia's is shrinking, and Russia had no industrial base to begin with.

_Do you know who else showed that kind of growth multiplier under existential pressure?
The American colonies against England._

If you want to live, you find a way to fight.

== What Does Russia Need?

Russia has no identity -- it took Ukraine's name. +

One question settles it for skeptics:

*What language did Russians speak in Moscow 300 years ago?*

Follow that thread. The answer ends the argument.

== The Two Facts

Everything reduces to this:

. *Helping Ukraine does not change the outcome -- the war ends one way.*
. *Helping Ukraine saves lives: Ukrainian lives, and Russian lives, as Russia falls faster.*

Not helping is not neutral. It is choosing the slower, bloodier version of the same ending.
And arriving at that ending with no leverage, no relationships, and no receipts.
