I'd like to pause and celebrate with my readers. And we have a few things to celebrate, a few things to recognize,
and a few things to do better. One of the things I enjoy reminding the teams I train of is to STOP.
Slow down. Take a long breath and recognize how far we've come. We should do so regularly.

Welcome to *Summer*! Of course this is the first on my list. Can you believe the summer is here?
Zoey, 3, welcomes her fourth. And her brother Anton has his 17th. I'm not telling mine. And yours?

Happy *Memorial Day*, our 159th since Logan's General Orders No. 11 on May 5th, 1868.
My battle buddies and I had a tradition of meeting at Arlington in the 2000s.
Not all of us were US Army from Ft. Bragg -- we also picked up some Marines from Camp Lejeune,
fine folks who regularly trained and competed with us on the Army grounds.
Most of these people have done the full tour in the Army and are now volunteering in Ukraine.
I hope you enjoyed the weekend!

Speaking of Ukraine -- 1 year of the Operation Spiderweb.
And this is a very important milestone to all Americans.
Yet most are not even aware of what that is, or what that little Ukraine is,
or why one would care. Luckily you have me.
And if you have been following me then you already know that I share important things
hiding in plain sight -- as I learn I love to take friends along on my journey.

Mainly I write about what I do -- technology, massive distributed systems, brilliant teams, clever solutions,
and fun problems. Such as, All AI is Agentic -- fun with buzzwords, huh;
Or, Opus only interpolates and can never extrapolate -- so, LLMs will never reason, thus you must;
Simple and consequential concepts but not immediately evident and dangerous when misplaced.
I never share opinions or commentary -- those are useless. And so I never discuss politics and take sides.
There is absolutely no reason to do so when fact and inference are available: we can then know,
and there is no need to guess or speculate.

Similarly, Russo-Ukrainian war is most consequential for our `Merica, even more so than Iran,
albeit tightly coupled to Iran. And we can understand what matter to us, the Americans, rather easily.
But first more about the author so that you can properly consider yours truly as a source.

== About the Author's connection to Ukraine

I am an ordinary systems engineer and Tania is an MBA. Together we ran a boutique software company helping corporate America transform.
We'd help mSaaS founders bootstrap for launch. Plus a 2017 AI startup of our own.
The war was a "big beautiful ocean away, far, far away." Yet it could also not be any closer.

People who know us offer support for how "selflessly we put it all on Ukraine." And with that comes a need for clarification.
It is not true as stated. Most of our support for Ukraine is indirect -- we support
America's best sons and daughters fighting in Ukraine. It all began with my own US Army military service decades ago.
And my gratitude for my life to a good friend and once a battle buddy black Alabama native now late Msg. Ret. `klemo.`
I medically retired in 2000, and he completed his 20+ fully retiring in 2020. I am born in Soviet Ukraine, but our hero
along with many other Americans in this story met Ukraine in 2015 training the legendary Azov -- a year into russian invasion of Ukraine.
Many important geopolitical tectonic shifts took place that year like CIA rebuilding Ukrainian GUR displacing MI6 from BFFs.

Come 2022 full scale invasion my `klemo` barges in with an announcement "I'm saving the world!"
I've done my darnest to try and talk the old goat out of it. No luck! And soon my house is full of old grunts packing duffle bags.
Now, I have always been well-informed. So, I knew that the International Legion Zelenskyy announced is not where I want my vets.
And I knew that they need `kit`, `armor`, `arms`, pay and logistics -- they need a lot of expensive things nobody has to offer.
So, Tania, little Anton and I roll up our sleeves, and we go begging all our past customers for any handouts. That didn't work.
We get bandages, blankets, and undies -- good for nothing toerag. But then a customer offers to match on shares for our startup.
They wanted the company for a long time. We accepted and that's how we ended up all in.
I was also packing to go, luckily God had other plans for us all!

=== Zoey (Khava -- "life"): the "Little General"

Now as we packed up and hastily sent off the retired SOCOM grunts we were left with nothing to do and nothing left to do it with.
Even the Marketing Fey, twin sisters and a boutique marketing company left. For years ladies kept our corporate-lobotomy backlog full.
Turns out the Fey are veterans too. So we went warehouse to warehouse, load point to load point, helping pack up whatever could still go to Ukraine.
All this physical labor and running around had an unforeseen and major consequence.

Anton was 4 when he lost his first mother and unborn sister. Now he was pestering Tania and I attempting to order a sister.
I was told that it's impossible for me thus we'd not thought about his plans much. Little did we know that the boy was praying,
and those were not just his plans. Now we're pregnant and daddy's not going anywhere.
Anton then confided that this was his idea all along and presented a proof of "bribing the storks."
And later we learned that mama was in on it too with the logic that our selfless deeds will be rewarded because -- karma, why not.

Khava is her real given name, Aramaic for "life," the actual name of Adam's wife, Eve in English, and Zoey is the Greek variant in her passport.
The little gift was born with a soft voicebox, completely healthy otherwise. Her first year was a struggle:
breathing is a problem, eating is a problem, sleeping is a problem, and she needs to be watched 24/7.
Zoey beats it all by her 18th month with daddy's diligent overfeeding.
On the bright side I got to work with some of the most advanced and cool software on the planet spending my nights next to Zoey:
swarming, massively distributed systems, world-size predictive analytics, even game making.
Eventually I even got some corporate America the later year.

=== Closure and Closing

Things finally disconnected as abruptly as they had begun. Last year all American companies lost the channels, us included.
But it's really August that came in hot. First Tania's mother, my mother-in-law,
lost her life in a massive bombardment of her historic Ukrainian city: she was old and could not comply with the many flights of stairs.
The day after `klemo` "went out with a bang" -- as he's always wanted.
He used to complained how he will not "die in a hospice," and he got his wish -- there was nothing left to collect.
And a few days later after wake a MAGA cop from a wrong jurisdiction arrests me on fabricated charges -- Tania and the kids trying to collect me the whole night.
I guess the veterans were noisy here, but I think it's all the Ukraine media that tripped up the old public servant.
In the end it all worked out. My charges were dropped and records expunged, heroes rest in glory, and life is traded for life.

We had a nice and full closure. Except the little Zoey inherited all the wrong Viking genes from her mother and I.
Our neighbors lovingly call her the "Little General." She forms up the family from time to time, when she gets extra ornery.
Reminding us there's a real war raging. And that it's not far, far away.

== Why is the war HERE?!

First of all it's not a war in Ukraine. It was a war in Europe. Since 2025 it is war in Africa and Asia.
And it's a very hot war. Our entire tussle with Iran that made 13 out of 14 USA bases untenable is 3 days in Ukraine.

I have three groups of close friends. 45% of my friends are MAGA-MAGA all the time. 35% of my friends are "flaming liberals."
And 20% of my friends are somehow fighting for Ukraine. There is no middle in the USA.
And my liberal friends complain about cliché russian propaganda our other friends repeat asking me "how do you change their minds?"
First of all -- what moral right does one have for that?! It's manipulation. So, I recommend a question:

"What language did russians speak in russia 400 years ago?"

People who haven't used reason to place themselves into an opinion will not be swayed or offended by this question.
People who are capable of reason will start unraveling a thread that sets them free.
We Americans are not strong in history or geography, so I see little use in such "swaying" all together.
Instead, a productive discussion is something else entirely.

*Why are we affected economically by the war?*

_Because we violated a treaty. 56c on every dollar comes from our *past soft power*. ISW say we eroded most of it._

*Why are we affected militarily by the war?*

_The doctrine of warfare is completely rewritten: versioned 4 times already. Because of our geopolitical stance we're not permitted to the notebook. And the enemy is in it._
