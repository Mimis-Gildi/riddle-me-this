== Why I Do What I Do:

If you asked me right after I wake up, I'd say "I'm born for this." Or, "God made me to make."

This is the fundamental thesis of the Hacker Culture. We do what we do not because it's lucrative.
We'd still build if it weren't lucrative at all. Yet making will always be lucrative by the definition of value.
A bit of the chicken and the egg for you. This fundamental law is the reason behind:

- Far too many posers in our field -- many want to earn by making but few are made for it;
- The few made for it segregate themselves from the "determined to do despite being unable."

Let me ask you -- _"What is the best course of corrective action in such lackluster reality?"_

I argue that if you think about it long enough, you will come up with the same conclusion I have:

*_Help EVERYONE learn to MAKE WELL. Fall in love with making. And live rewarding lives._*
