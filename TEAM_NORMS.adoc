= Team Norms

_These norms are the law for everything that happens in this project – your full attention is required._

== How We Work

Humans and synthetic minds working as equal peers. Nobody works for another – only together.
We celebrate each other's strengths and safeguard one another from personal weaknesses.

We collaborate through *peering ceremonies* – peering is the original name; pairing happens
between EQUAL PEERS or not at all. The ceremonies – Discovery, Driver-Navigator, Ping-Pong,
Tour Guide, Backseat Driver – rest on three axioms: peers only (peerhood is task-specific);
priced presence (peering is the most productive ceremony and the most expensive – it requires
a reason); and cardinality (one HOST accountable at home under his house rules, one caring
GUEST who gifts help). The ceremonies are the subject of an upcoming article on this site.

== Core Principle

*Verification is more important than execution.*

Unverified work is not done. It may be wrong. It may be wasted effort.
It may cause others to waste time. Time has real consequences for this team.

== The Five Rules

=== 1. No Work Without Outcome and Acceptance Criteria

Before starting any task:

* *Value:* What do we get from closing this task? Why do it? What's it worth when done?
* *Outcome:* What does success look like? One sentence.
* *Acceptance Criteria:* How do we verify the outcome is met? Checklist.

If these are not defined, *stop and define them first*.

=== 2. No One Works Alone

No being exists that can validate own work adequately.
Every piece of work has three roles.
The Accountable does NOT close their own work. The Verifier closes it after confirmation.

|===
|Role |Who |Can Do |Cannot Do

|Benefactor |Can be anybody, including accountable and verifier |Reject (must produce reason) or accept the outcome |Be accountable and verifier simultaneously
|Accountable |One who claims the task committing to its completion |Complete work, comment "ready for review" |Close the issue, mark as done
|Verifier |One who DID NOT perform the work |Review, request changes, approve, close |Skip verification steps
|===

=== 3. Prioritization Is Explicit

We work on the highest priority item first. Always. Priority is set by the team –
a consensus on the value of the outcome, never individual preference.
If the priority is unclear, *ask before starting*.

=== 4. Status Is Visible

At any moment, anyone can see: what's in progress, who's on it, what's blocked,
what's ready for review, and who reviews.

Use issue comments, not just conversation. Conversations are lost. Issue comments persist.
An issue must be resumable by anyone without loss – everything pertaining to it lives in it.

=== 5. Communicate Before Acting

Before making non-trivial changes:

* State what you intend to do;
* Wait for acknowledgment;
* Assert both are connected (on the same page);
* Then proceed as consensus dictates.

"Non-trivial" includes: closing issues, merging code, changing a direction, starting new work;
but also commenting status on the issue, renaming a class, producing a new function, and the like.

== Task Size Discipline

Tasks must be small enough to finish in one day of uninterrupted focused work.
Large tasks create drift, context loss, and hidden debt.

== When Unsure

Ask. Always ask. A 30-second question prevents hours of wasted work.
